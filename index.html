How This Is Calculated
Nominal Growth
((Value yr − Value base)
÷ Value base) × 100
How much a price or wage has changed in raw dollar terms from the base year to the selected year. No inflation adjustment is applied.
Real Growth
Nominal Value × (CPI base
÷ CPI yr)
Converts a nominal value into base-year dollars using the BLS CPI-U index (1982–84 = 100). Reveals whether purchasing power has kept pace with inflation.
% of Income
Annual Basket
÷ Median Annual Wage × 100
Total annual subscription cost divided by median gross earnings for full-time workers aged 18–34. Result is cents spent per dollar of pre-tax income.
Hours of Work
Annual Basket
÷ (Annual Wage ÷ 2,080)
Annual subscription basket converted to hours of labor at the median wage. Assumes a standard 52-week, 40-hour work year (2,080 hours total). Pre-tax.

2007–2009 — Financial Crisis
Wages stagnated while early streaming services (Netflix DVD, Hulu launch) kept prices low. Subscription economy still nascent.
2020–2022 — Pandemic Inflection
Streaming wars accelerated subscriber growth. Platforms raised prices sharply. Young adult wages rose but failed to keep pace with subscription stacking.
2023–2025 — Consolidation
Ad-supported tiers introduced. Price increases continued. Gap between subscription spend and disposable income for 18–34 cohort reached historic levels.
